Spectral distribution¶

The source spectrum is selected from predefined spectra (black-body or power-law), available libraries of stellar and non-stellar spectra, a user-defined spectra and an emission line can also be added.

Stellar library¶

The Pickles (1998; PASP, 110, 863) stellar library covers optical and near-IR wavelengths out to 2.5um across a broad range of spectral types (O5 to M9) and luminosity classes (I, III and V).

Non-Stellar library¶

Elliptical galaxy - a template spectrum (class T=-5, -4) covering the wavelength range 22 - 10000 nm and taken from the Pegase Atlas of Galaxies by Fioc & Rocca-Volmerange (1997). Spiral galaxy QSO - QSO2 - HII region - covers the wavelength range 100 - 1100 nm and was taken from the HST STIS exposure time calculator. The spectrum has a sampling of 0.05nm. NGC 7009 - covers the wavelength range 100 - 1100 nm and was taken from the HST STIS exposure time calculator. The spectrum has a sampling of 0.05nm. IC5117 covers the wavelength range 480 - 2500 nm and is taken from correspondence with Rick Rudy, based on Rudy et al. (2001, AJ, 121, 362). The spectrum has a sampling of 0.05nm.

Source Brightness¶

The source flux density is specified in magnitudes at a given band. The source spectrum is scaled using these value with the corresponding spectral energy distribution. An insterstellar extinction curve can be applied (the curve … will be used) [NOT YET AVAILABLE]. The spectral energy distribution can be shifted in wavelength using either a redshift value or a radial velocity.
